# Ethical Considerations in AI-Assisted Development

> A research-grounded guide to the ethical, social, and environmental dimensions of using AI in software development.

## Why This Matters

As of 2025, 84% of developers are using or planning to use AI tools in their development process (Stack Overflow Developer Survey 2025). AI coding assistants are no longer experimental -- they are infrastructure. With that scale comes responsibility: to the developers whose work trains these systems, to the junior engineers whose career paths are being reshaped, to the communities who bear the environmental costs, and to the end users who interact with the code these tools produce.

This collection examines the ethical dimensions that every team using AI coding tools should understand and address.

## Key Principles

1. **Human accountability is non-negotiable.** AI generates suggestions; humans ship code. The person who accepts AI output owns it.
2. **Transparency builds trust.** Disclose AI usage in code, documentation, and team processes.
3. **Skill development must not be sacrificed for speed.** Especially for junior developers, understanding trumps velocity.
4. **Environmental costs are real and measurable.** Every AI query has an energy cost; optimize accordingly.
5. **Bias is inherited, not invented.** AI models reflect their training data. Active testing and diverse review are required.
6. **Access should expand, not contract.** AI tools should lower barriers for underrepresented groups, not create new gatekeeping.
